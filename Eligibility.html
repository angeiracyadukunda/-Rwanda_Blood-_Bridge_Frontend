Eligibility Requirements

Before you donate blood, it's important to make sure you meet the necessary requirements. Here are some general eligibility guidelines you should consider.

General Eligibility Requirements

To ensure the safety of both donors and recipients, donors must meet certain criteria. Here are the general eligibility requirements you need to know before donating blood:

  • Age: Must be at least 18 years old (16-17 with parental consent).
  • Weight: Minimum weight of 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • Health: Must be in good general health and feeling well.
  • Medical History: No recent history of certain diseases or medical conditions.

Health and Medical History

Blood donation is a simple, safe process, but not everyone is eligible to donate. Some conditions may temporarily or permanently prevent you from donating blood. Here are some specific considerations:

  • Travel: Recent travel to certain countries may affect eligibility.
  • Medications: Certain medications may prevent you from donating.
  • Tattoos and Piercings: You may need to wait a period after getting a tattoo or piercing.
  • Pregnancy: Women who are pregnant or recently pregnant may not be eligible.
